Case Study

Devolved Political Campaign Operations in Wales

Hands-on organisation, mobilisation, and bilingual delivery for Welsh political campaigns during a period of exceptional democratic intensity.

Context Personal / Advisory Capacity
Sector Politics & Devolved Democracy
Capabilities Secretariat, Mobilisation, Bilingual Delivery
Context

Campaigning in a devolved political landscape

Wales in 2015 to 2016 saw an unusually concentrated period of democratic activity. The Women's Equality Party was preparing for its inaugural Senedd election campaign, and the EU referendum mobilised cross-party groups across the country. Both required disciplined campaign operations delivered bilingually, at speed, and across multiple locations in Wales.

The challenge was not ideological, it was operational. These campaigns needed someone who could organise logistics, coordinate volunteers, manage candidate schedules, produce bilingual materials, and keep activity running consistently on the ground across Wales.

Provenance note: This work was undertaken by Hannah Van Den Bergh in a personal and advisory capacity, prior to the founding of VDB. It is included here because the operational and organisational skills demonstrated are directly relevant to the programme delivery and secretariat capabilities that VDB offers today.

Objective

Keeping political campaigns organised and visible on the ground

To provide the operational backbone for two distinct political campaigns in Wales, ensuring that candidate support, event logistics, volunteer coordination, and bilingual public engagement were delivered reliably and at scale.

This meant translating political ambition into practical, day-to-day campaign infrastructure: schedules that worked, materials that arrived on time, events that ran smoothly, and a consistent presence across Welsh communities.

Translating political ambition into practical, day-to-day campaign infrastructure that could operate bilingually across Wales.

Why It Mattered

Political campaigns depend on operational discipline

In a devolved political landscape, campaigns face a distinctive set of operational challenges. Activity must happen bilingually. It must respect the specific dynamics of Welsh constituencies and communities. And it must be coordinated across multiple locations simultaneously, often with limited resources and tight timelines.

The difference between a visible, credible campaign and a disorganised one is almost always operational, not ideological.

The difference between a visible, credible campaign and a disorganised one is almost always operational. Someone has to manage the schedules, book the venues, coordinate the volunteers, prepare the materials, and make sure everything arrives where it needs to be. This work demonstrated that disciplined programme management, the same capability that drives complex funded projects, is just as critical in political campaign delivery.

Outcome

Operational discipline for public-facing campaigns

Campaign activity was coordinated consistently across multiple locations in Wales. Candidates received structured support. Events ran on schedule. Bilingual materials reached communities in both Welsh and English. Cross-party coordination, often the hardest part of multi-stakeholder political work, was maintained throughout.

The lasting value of this experience is the proof that the operational and organisational disciplines needed for political campaign delivery, secretariat functions, event logistics, bilingual communications, volunteer coordination, are the same disciplines that underpin effective programme management in any complex, multi-stakeholder environment.
