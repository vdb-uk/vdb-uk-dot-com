Case Study

Multi-Partner MedTech Programme Delivery for Paediatric Rehabilitation

Coordinating an Innovate UK-funded collaboration between GripAble, Imperial College London, and NHS clinical partners to develop the SqueezAble device for children with cerebral palsy.

Client GripAble Ltd
Sector MedTech & Rehabilitation
Capabilities Programme Management, Partner Coordination, Grant Delivery
Funding £800k Innovate UK BMC
Context

An Imperial spinout scaling smart rehabilitation

GripAble, a spinout from Imperial College London, had developed a smart handheld rehabilitation device already deployed in over 350 healthcare locations across the UK. The device was transforming how clinicians delivered upper-limb rehabilitation, combining soft-sensor technology with gamified therapy to improve patient engagement and outcomes.

Winning an £800k Innovate UK Biomedical Catalyst grant opened the door to developing SqueezAble, a next-generation soft-sensor device designed specifically for children with cerebral palsy. But delivering a grant-funded, multi-partner R&D programme involving a startup, a world-class university lab, and NHS clinical teams required a different kind of expertise: structured programme management that could hold the complexity together.

Objective

Delivering a grant-funded medtech programme on time and on track

VDB was brought in to provide programme management for the SqueezAble project, coordinating delivery across GripAble's product team, Imperial College's Department of Bioengineering, and NHS clinical partners.

The challenge was not just technical. It was about aligning different institutional cultures, managing funder expectations, and ensuring that an ambitious research programme maintained the pace and rigour needed to meet Innovate UK milestones, while keeping the end user, children with cerebral palsy, at the centre of every decision.

Holding complexity together: aligning a startup, a university lab, and NHS clinical teams around a shared programme of grant-funded R&D.

What We Delivered

End-to-end programme delivery across three partners

01

Programme & Milestone Management

Managed the full SqueezAble programme across GripAble, Imperial College's Department of Bioengineering, and NHS clinical partners. Tracked technical milestones, coordinated workstreams, and compiled progress reports for Innovate UK, ensuring compliance and deliverable tracking throughout the £800k grant lifecycle.

02

Multi-Partner Coordination

Facilitated collaboration between Dr Firat Guder's research group (soft-sensor technology at Imperial) and GripAble's product team. Managed clinical input from Imperial College Healthcare NHS Trust and coordinated occupational therapy expertise, bridging the gap between academic research, commercial product development, and frontline clinical need.

03

Funder Communications & Reporting

Maintained ongoing funder relationship with Innovate UK. Prepared and submitted milestone reports, managed budget spend tracking, and handled escalation processes, keeping the programme on solid footing with its primary funding body.

04

Risk & Budget Governance

Maintained a risk register and escalation framework across the consortium. Monitored budget allocation and spend against Innovate UK requirements, ensuring that resources were deployed effectively and that any emerging risks were identified and addressed early.

05

Clinical Integration & User-Centred Focus

Ensured that clinical insight from NHS occupational therapists and rehabilitation specialists remained central to the development process. Coordinated user testing and clinical feedback loops so that SqueezAble was shaped by the people who would use it, therapists and children alike.

Why It Mattered

Grant-funded medtech needs more than good science

Multi-partner medtech programmes carry a particular kind of risk. The science may be excellent, the clinical need proven, the funding secured, but without structured programme delivery, even the most promising collaborations can lose momentum, miss milestones, or fail to translate research into real-world impact.

The difference between a funded project and a delivered one is programme management that holds the complexity together, across institutions, disciplines, and timelines.

Our work ensured that GripAble's SqueezAble programme stayed on track: milestones were met, partners remained aligned, funder requirements were satisfied, and clinical insight informed every stage. We brought the programme management discipline that allowed good science to become a viable, impactful product.

Outcome

From funded project to global medtech platform

The SqueezAble programme was delivered successfully, with all Innovate UK milestones met and the grant fully executed. But the impact went far beyond the funded project itself. GripAble went on to raise an $11M Series A round, expand into the US market through a partnership with Medline, and reach over 50% of UK stroke care pathways.

The structured programme management that VDB provided during the critical early phase helped establish the operational rigour and credibility that supported GripAble's subsequent growth, from a funded research collaboration to a globally recognised rehabilitation technology company.
