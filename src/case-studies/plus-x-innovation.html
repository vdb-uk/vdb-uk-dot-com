Case Study

Designing a National Hardware Accelerator Programme

How VDB shaped the accelerator at the heart of Plus X Innovation's national hub network, launching 69 companies, creating 1,100+ jobs, and unlocking £180m in investment.

Client Plus X Innovation
Sector Hardware & Innovation
Capabilities Research & Innovation, Funding Strategy, Programme Design
Context

Where hardware startups meet infrastructure

Hardware and product startups face a fundamentally different challenge from their software counterparts. They need physical prototyping facilities, access to engineering mentors, supply chain guidance, and patient capital, not just desk space and Wi-Fi. In the UK, this segment was significantly underserved, with few purpose-built accelerator programmes designed around the specific needs of hardware founders.

Plus X Innovation was building a national network of innovation hubs to address this gap, starting with the Central Research Laboratory (CRL) on the former EMI R&D campus in Hayes, West London. They needed a partner to design the accelerator programme that would sit at the heart of this network, one that could launch companies, attract investment, and scale nationally.

Objective

Build the programme, map the ecosystem, plan for scale

VDB was engaged to design a hardware accelerator programme that could operate at the centre of a national innovation hub network. The brief was threefold: create an accelerator structure and curriculum that worked for hardware founders, map the innovation ecosystem to identify the right partners and mentors, and inform the scaling strategy as the network expanded beyond London.

This was not a consulting exercise at arm's length. We embedded within the team, working alongside Plus X, Brunel University London, and developer U+I to shape a programme that would be credible to investors, valuable to founders, and replicable across multiple sites.

Design an accelerator that could launch hardware companies, attract institutional investment, and scale from one hub to a national network.

What We Delivered

From programme architecture to national expansion

01

Accelerator Programme Design

Designed the 18-week CRL Accelerator structure and curriculum from the ground up. Defined selection criteria, cohort size, and progression milestones. Created the complete support framework including mentorship pathways, prototyping facility access, and funding guidance for hardware founders.

02

Innovation Ecosystem Mapping

Identified and connected the key partners, mentors, and institutional supporters needed to make the programme credible and effective. Mapped the UK hardware startup landscape, identified unmet needs, and benchmarked against international hardware accelerators to ensure world-class programme design.

03

Scaling & Expansion Strategy

Informed the expansion strategy as Plus X grew from Hayes to Brighton and Slough. Designed programme variants tailored to different stages, including BOOST for early-stage ventures and the Stage One Pre-Accelerator, ensuring the model could flex across locations and founder maturity levels.

04

Funding & Grant Pathway Development

Developed the funding guidance framework that helped cohort companies access £25m in grants. Created pathways connecting founders with public funding bodies, angel networks, and institutional investors, contributing to the portfolio companies collectively raising £180m.

05

National Programme Impact Assessment

Contributed to the evidence base demonstrating the programme's national impact: 69 companies supported across 10+ accelerator cohorts (2017 to 2023), 1,100+ jobs created, and recognition through the Property Week Awards. This impact data supported the case for continued expansion and institutional backing.

Why It Mattered

Hardware founders need more than desk space

The UK's innovation infrastructure was built primarily for software and digital businesses. Hardware startups, building physical products that require prototyping, testing, manufacturing partnerships, and longer development cycles, were largely left without the dedicated support they needed. Generic accelerator programmes often failed these founders because they didn't account for the realities of hardware development.

We designed a programme that treated hardware founders as what they are: engineers building real things that need real infrastructure, real mentors, and patient capital.

By designing a programme specifically for hardware, we helped create an environment where physical product startups could develop at the same pace and with the same ambition as their software peers. The result was not just a successful accelerator, but a replicable model that has now expanded to three locations and supported companies that have gone on to raise significant follow-on investment.

Outcome

From one hub to a national network

The accelerator programme we designed became the centrepiece of Plus X Innovation's national network. From CRL's launch in Hayes in 2015, the model has scaled to Brighton (2020) and Slough (2023), with 10+ cohorts delivered and Cohort 12 now underway. The programme has directly supported 69 companies, helped founders access £25m in grants, and contributed to portfolio companies collectively raising £180m in investment.

With over 1,100 jobs created and recognition through the Property Week Awards, the programme stands as evidence that well-designed, sector-specific accelerator infrastructure can deliver outsized impact, when the programme design is right from the start.
