Case Study

Sustainability Research and Enterprise Ecosystem Design Through Cambridge

Delivering applied research, plastics analysis, and international enterprise model design through the Cambridge Institute for Sustainability Leadership.

Institution Cambridge Institute for Sustainability Leadership
Sector Sustainability & Materials
Capabilities Research, ESG & Sustainability, Science & Engineering
Context

Applied sustainability research through a world-leading institution

Over several years, Hannah Van Den Bergh was profiled as a researcher through the Cambridge Institute for Sustainability Leadership (CISL), operating under the University of Cambridge brand. This connection provided the institutional credibility and research infrastructure needed to tackle complex sustainability questions at scale, from materials and supply chain analysis to international enterprise design.

The work spanned multiple assignments across UK and international contexts, combining analytical rigour with practical delivery for corporate, institutional, and multilateral audiences. It demonstrated VDB's capacity to operate at the intersection of research, sustainability strategy, and systems-level enterprise thinking.

Objective

Translating complexity into usable intelligence

The challenge across each assignment was the same: to take deeply technical or structurally complex sustainability questions and produce research, analysis, and recommendations that organisations could act on.

This required not only subject-matter expertise in materials, circular economy, and sustainability, but also the programme management capability to coordinate multi-stakeholder research and deliver outputs under a globally recognised institutional brand.

Delivering credible, actionable research under the Cambridge brand, bridging the gap between academic rigour and practical application.

What We Delivered

Research, analysis, and enterprise design across sectors

01

Materials & Plastics Research

Detailed analysis of plastics supply chains, waste management, films, and flexible packaging for organisations including Sky, the British Plastics Federation, and Mondelez, translating complexity into usable reports and advocacy tools.

02

Multi-Part Research Programme Management

Managed complex, multi-part research assignments under the Cambridge brand, combining analytical rigour with practical delivery across stakeholder groups, sectors, and reporting formats.

03

International Enterprise Ecosystem Design

Senior advisory and research on the design of a hub-and-spoke accelerator model for sustainable and climate-led innovation across Islamic nation states, working across questions of policy, language, GDP context, institutional readiness, and international partnership.

04

Corporate Sustainability Advisory

Research and analysis delivered in partnership with major corporates including Microsoft and Amazon, applying the CISL lens to industry-specific sustainability challenges and translating findings into strategic recommendations.

Why It Mattered

Research credibility requires both depth and institutional standing

Sustainability research that changes behaviour, whether corporate, policy, or institutional, needs to do more than produce data. It needs to be trusted, interpreted, and delivered in a way that makes sense to the people who will use it. The Cambridge affiliation provided that trust; our team provided the analytical depth and practical delivery.

Combining institutional credibility with hands-on research delivery, producing work that was both rigorous and genuinely useful.

The international enterprise work, in particular, demonstrated the ability to operate across cultural, economic, and political boundaries, designing innovation systems that needed to work in radically different contexts. This kind of work requires not just technical skill but genuine sensitivity to context, partnership, and institutional design.

Outcome

Research that informed strategy, advocacy, and innovation design

Across these assignments, our work delivered research that directly informed corporate sustainability strategies, sector-wide advocacy, and the architecture of international innovation ecosystems. The outputs were used by organisations including Sky, the British Plastics Federation, Mondelez, Microsoft, and Amazon, as well as multilateral stakeholders working on climate-led enterprise development.

The lasting value was not only in the individual reports and recommendations, but in demonstrating a model for how applied sustainability research can be delivered with both institutional credibility and real-world practical impact, a model that continues to inform VDB's approach to complex research commissions today.
